# CAN-004 – Intellectual Lineage

Status: Draft

## Purpose

Intellectual lineage records the ideas, traditions, systems, and engineering cultures that influenced AES, Project Zero, Catalyst, Atarix, and related work.

It exists to preserve context without turning influence into unquestioned authority.

## Principle

Influence is evidence, not command.

A prior system, paper, tool, culture, or engineering tradition may guide current work, but it does not automatically settle current design questions.

Every inherited idea must remain subject to observation, understanding, and improvement.

## Why Lineage Matters

Engineering decisions are easier to maintain when future contributors can understand where assumptions came from.

Lineage helps explain:

- why a design feels familiar
- what prior systems were considered
- what tradeoffs were inherited
- what mistakes should not be repeated
- what vocabulary carries historical meaning

## Recording Influence

When recording intellectual lineage, capture:

1. The source or tradition being referenced.
2. The relevant idea, method, pattern, or warning.
3. How it influenced the present work.
4. Whether the influence is adopted, adapted, rejected, or under study.
5. Any limits or risks in applying it.

## Categories of Lineage

AES lineage may include, but is not limited to:

- operating system design traditions
- hardware architecture traditions
- programming language and tooling traditions
- documentation and standards traditions
- safety and reliability engineering
- research methodology
- open-source governance
- personal engineering experience
- historical computing systems

## Traceability

Lineage entries should support traceability, not nostalgia.

If an influence affects a standard, decision record, architecture, glossary term, template, or research direction, the affected document should reference the lineage entry or explain the relationship directly.

## Non-Authority Clause

No lineage source is sacred.

The value of lineage is to make influence visible so that the project can reason about it honestly.

Visibility improves judgment. It does not replace judgment.
