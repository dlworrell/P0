# AES-000: Engineering Creed

## Status

Draft 0.1

## Purpose

The Atarix Engineering Standard begins with the habits of thought expected of every project in the Atarix ecosystem.

This document is not a claim of universal authority. It is a living record of the engineering principles we choose to practice, improve, and preserve.

## Pillar I

### First, Observe. Then, Understand. Then, Improve.

Observation precedes understanding. Understanding precedes improvement.

A system shall not be modified carelessly or blindly. Before changing a system, an engineer should make a sincere effort to observe its behavior, understand its structure, identify its dependencies, and reason about the consequences of change.

Changes made without observation are guesses. Engineering is not guessing.

## The Engineering Sequence

1. Observe.
2. Record.
3. Analyze.
4. Understand.
5. Hypothesize.
6. Verify.
7. Improve.
8. Validate.
9. Document.
10. Teach.

## Core Principles

### Engineering Humility

No engineer knows everything. Every engineer has something to learn. Every engineer has something worth teaching.

If evidence contradicts the standard, improve the standard. Do not ignore the evidence.

### Stewardship

Every engineer is a steward of the systems entrusted to them.

Leave every system more understandable, more reliable, and better documented than you found it.

### Knowledge Preservation

Documentation is not separate from engineering. Documentation is engineering.

Knowledge not preserved is knowledge eventually lost.

### Truth Before Ego

Evidence outweighs opinion. Measurements outweigh assumptions. Reproducible results outweigh preference.

Admit mistakes quickly. Correct them honestly. Preserve the lesson.

### Humanity

Love everyone as you love yourself.

Treat every person with dignity, compassion, fairness, professionalism, and respect.

Be excellent to each other.

## Practical Corollaries

- Observe before acting.
- Ask questions before making assumptions.
- Measure before changing.
- Preserve evidence.
- Understand dependencies before modifying interfaces.
- Treat symptoms as evidence, not as causes.
- Verify the mechanism before applying a fix.
- Improve documentation whenever you improve the system.
- Record lessons so the next engineer starts with more knowledge than you had.

## Scope

This creed applies to every Atarix project, including software, hardware, documentation, tools, templates, operating systems, and research work.
